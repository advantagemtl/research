Interpretation

Baby vs. Bats boils down to two classic PUA archetypes clashing:

Baby: Raw, outcome-driven, impatient. He grinds in the dirt — solo daygame, ugly sarging to kill ego, forcing scary approaches, judging progress by real results (lays). Ruthless with himself, so ruthless with anyone dodging the same pain.

Bats: Theory-loaded, process-focused, indirect. Hides behind props (magic tricks, Mystery scripts, group nightgame), dropped $5,500 on workshops, brags volume but admits zero lays and says it’s not even the goal. Textbook permanent intermediate.

Neither is fully “right,” but Baby’s path is the one that actually leads somewhere.

Baby’s rude as hell — the digs about smell, handshake, necklace, stalking ex, fake body count are harsh and borderline mean. Some of it’s projection: he’s pissed at his own past delays, so he unloads on the guy who mirrors the traps he’s escaping.

But the core judgment isn’t too harsh. Bats screams red flags:

• Big money on theory, no results
• Volume bragging with zero sexual intent
• Props and groups as crutches
• Trashing daygame while avoiding its exposure
• Defensive “you’re too outcome-oriented” cope

Baby’s read — “ego-shielding via framework to avoid action” — is dead accurate. Guys stuck forever look exactly like Bats.

Baby uses him perfectly: temporary tool for the wing effect, discard when no longer needed. Cold, but smart momentum protection.

Bottom line: Baby’s edge comes from hunger; Bats’ niceness from comfort. Hunger wins, even if it turns you into a bit of an asshole.
